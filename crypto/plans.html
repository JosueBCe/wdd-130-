This strategy is based on the nature of the stock market cycle, and capitalizes on the expected fluctuations in stock market prices. Buy low, sell high is a strategy where you buy stocks or securities at a low price and sell them at a higher price.


Having a “long” position in a security means that you own the security. Investors maintain “long” security positions in the expectation that the stock will rise in value in the future. The opposite of a “long” position is a “short” position. A "short" position is generally the sale of a stock you do not own. Investors who sell short believe the price of the stock will decrease in value. If the price drops, you can buy the stock at the lower price and make a profit. If the price of the stock rises and you buy it back later at the higher price, you will incur a loss. Short selling is for the experienced investor.
